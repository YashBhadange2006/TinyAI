# Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

TinyAI is designed to run locally on your device. This policy explains what data is stored, what may leave the device, and what you control.

## What the app stores locally

- Chat messages and chat session history
- Selected system prompts
- Downloaded model files
- Attached images and other locally saved media
- App settings such as theme and model preferences

## What the app does not collect

- We do not run user accounts.
- We do not intentionally collect analytics or advertising identifiers.
- We do not sell personal data.
- We do not upload your chat history to our own servers.

## Network use

The app can use the internet only when you choose to:

- browse or fetch model listings from Hugging Face
- download model files from model repositories

When you download a model, the app contacts the selected model host so the file can be retrieved. The model provider may receive normal network information such as your IP address, device request metadata, and download timing.

## On-device processing

Text generation and image inference are performed locally on your device using supported on-device runtimes. Your prompts and responses remain on the device unless you manually share them.

## File permissions and media

If you attach an image, the app reads the selected file and may store a local copy so it can be referenced in chat history. The app only accesses files you explicitly choose.

## Data retention

Your chats and downloaded files stay on the device until you delete them or uninstall the app.

## Third-party services and libraries

The app uses third-party open-source libraries and may contact Hugging Face for model downloads. Those services have their own terms and privacy practices.

## Children

This app is not intended for children under 13.

## Changes

We may update this policy if app behavior changes. The latest version should always be included with the app or linked from its store listing.

## Contact

If you have questions about this policy, contact the developer through the project page.
